# Tone And Style Standard

## Principles alignment

- Skimmable: structure for fast scanning.
- Consistent: keep one canonical term per concept.
- Participatory: write for both contributors and readers.
- Current: remove or update phrasing that no longer matches product behavior.

See [principles.md](./principles.md) for the full principle taxonomy.

## Audience split

- End-user docs: friendly, casual, helpful, benefit-aware.
- Technical and contributor docs: precise, direct, implementation-focused.

## Voice rules

- Use second person for user docs: "you" and "your".
- Use active voice.
- Prefer short sentences in procedures.
- Explain the why before deep how for conceptual sections.

## Do and do not

Do:

- Start with outcome-focused statements.
- Use concrete examples with realistic values.
- Keep paragraphs short (2 to 5 lines).

Do not:

- Use condescending words like "simply" or "obviously".
- Hide key behavior in long narrative blocks.
- Use inconsistent terms for the same concept.

## Inclusive language

- Default to gender-neutral wording and role nouns.
- Prefer respectful, specific language over loaded or dismissive words.
- Avoid ableist phrasing in instructions and troubleshooting.
- Keep error and warning text constructive, direct, and actionable.

Examples:

- Use "allowlist" and "blocklist" instead of "whitelist" and "blacklist".
- Use "This can fail if..." instead of "Only beginners hit this error".

For full policy and reviewer prompts, see [accessibility-and-bias.md](./accessibility-and-bias.md).

## Global audience and plain language

- Prefer plain language over idioms and culture-specific references.
- Expand uncommon acronyms on first use.
- Avoid assumptions about reader timezone, locale, budget, or platform.
- Keep sentence structure predictable for localization and machine translation.

Prefer:

- "accomplish two tasks at once" over animal-violence idioms.
- concrete time references like "UTC" when scheduling matters.

## Readability limits

- Keep heading depth to H3 in most pages.
- Keep bullets flat and scannable.
- Use tables for structured comparisons, not prose.

## Terminology policy

- Pick one canonical term per concept.
- Keep a glossary page in Reference.
- Avoid synonyms for command names, flags, and API fields.
